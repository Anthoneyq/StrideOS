## 4. The insight-delivery play — one backtested placement call BEFORE any payment link

Run this the moment either coach sends a roster. The insight goes out with **no payment link in the same message**.

1. **Get the roster in.** Ask for an Athletic.net export or just pasted times. Use the roster paste/import in the app. Kids with 2+ events across different distances are where the engine can personalize; single-PR kids fall back to the standard curve — don't build the headline insight on a single-PR kid.

2. **Read the roster.** Open the roster's Event Fit / Event Finder view. List every athlete whose best-scoring event per the curve differs from the event they're actually being entered in. Sanity-check candidates against the lineup screen. (Note honestly: the current lineup assignment is heuristic — it doesn't yet model opponents or full meet scoring, so the insight is "this kid fits this event better," not "this lineup wins the meet.")

3. **Backtest before you send.** Open the Proof Ledger on their roster: hold-one-out against their own kids' real times, error next to Riegel and VDOT. Only send an insight the ledger supports on that roster. If the ledger looks weak on their kids, say so and send the most defensible finding instead — with Doug especially, an honest miss beats a shaky claim.

4. **Pick ONE insight.** The single most non-obvious, highest-stakes placement call — ideally a points swing ("X is entered in the 800 where he's mid-pack for district; his curve says he places in the 1600"). One, not five. Five is a sales deck; one is a coach talking to a coach.

5. **Send it plain.** Two or three sentences plus one screenshot: the call, the reasoning in one line, and the backtest error on their own kids. Close with "want me to run the whole roster before your next meet?" — not a checkout link. The founding ask (day 7 message) only goes out after this lands, and only once the coupon gate below is cleared.
