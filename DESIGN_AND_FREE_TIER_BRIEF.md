# StrideOS — Design + Free-Tier Implementation Brief

*Source: multi-agent research workflow (competitor free-tiers, anthoneyq.com design DNA, motion stack, live-vs-new audit), 2026-06-29.*

## Executive summary

StrideOS already shares anthoneyq.com's most important genes (the #0B0D0F base, glass, ambient glow, eyebrow signature, --ease) and has correctly swapped blue→STRIDE orange. The redesign's real failures are structural, not aesthetic: a cold coach is dropped straight into an instrument panel with no pitch, auth is a 9–11px sidebar-footer widget showing dev-speak ('Local Mode'), and the one feature that beats every free competitor — roster-level Squad Intelligence — is hidden behind a locked teaser even though the compute and render functions already exist. This brief makes five decisive P0 moves: (1) change the trial to 30 days in all four places (UI copy index.html:958, a new CREATE-OR-REPLACE migration for upsert_coach_profile's interval, the still-unscheduled expire_trials pg_cron, and an optional +16d backfill); (2) build a compact signed-out 'home' front door that puts a hero + CTA ladder directly ABOVE a live free experience rather than a marketing scroll-story; (3) un-gate a real, capped (≤6-athlete, view-only) Squad Intelligence taste with a pre-loaded demo squad and 60-second bulk paste — the screenshot-worthy wow no free tool offers — moving the PRO wall to scale, save/sync, environmental adjustment and PDF export; (4) kill the dev-speak status copy; and (5) promote auth into a readable centered modal. P1 follows with the typography fix (keep Fraunces as the distinctive display voice but add Inter for readable body and floor mono at 11px — resolving the Inter-vs-Fraunces conflict in StrideOS's favor without losing its soul) and a layered, performance-safe motion stack: GSAP only for hero choreography and KPI count-ups, native View Transitions for view swaps, CSS scroll-driven reveals, and a landing-only WebGL accent — all under a strict reduced-motion guard and compositor-only rule so the data-heavy SPA never drops frames. Net: a premium anthoneyq sibling that delivers genuine value before any ask and out-features every free calculator coaches use today.

**Typography**
- DISPLAY — Fraunces (headlines + big result/score numbers): bump weight 300→500/600, tighten tracking ~-.04em. This serif is StrideOS's own differentiator vs anthoneyq's Inter — KEEP it but make it confident, not thin.
- BODY/UI — ADD Inter (or -apple-system stack) as the readable human font for body copy, nav, buttons, auth, paragraphs. Restores the anthoneyq family gene (Inter) AND fixes the audit's #1 failure: body is currently Fraunces serif (index.html:56).
- DATA/EYEBROW — JetBrains Mono restricted to data, stats, eyebrows, micro-labels with an 11px (.6875rem) FLOOR — eliminate every 9px/.5625rem use. Mono is for numbers, not sentences.
- Eyebrow signature: uppercase, .12–.2em tracking, preceded by a glowing 7px orange dot (anthoneyq tell, accent-swapped) — KEEP, it is the brand fingerprint.

> Decisive typography ruling: the DNA research's 'Inter everywhere' and the local build's 'Fraunces+JetBrains' conflict — resolve by keeping Fraunces as the distinctive display voice (don't throw away the redesign's soul) while adding Inter as the readable body/UI layer and demoting mono to data-only. This satisfies both the family DNA and the audit's readability gap in one move. Reuse anthoneyq glass-card gradient-hairline trick, ambient radial-glow + masked 64px grid, --ease cubic-bezier(.22,.61,.36,1), and 18px/12px/999px radius vocabulary — they already exist in the file.

## Free-tier plan
- **[P0/medium]** Convert Squad Intelligence from a locked teaser to a REAL, capped free taste. Replace the squadAnalyticsLocked() call (index.html ~2796) with the real renderSquadAnalytics(computeSquadAnalytics(...)) output for free/anonymous coaches, capped to ≤6 athletes, view-only (no save/sync), with a StrideOS-branded printable. Both compute + render functions ALREADY EXIST.
  - _Why:_ This is the single biggest wedge: NO free competitor does roster-level prediction (McMillan/VDOT/Tinman are single-athlete; athletic.net has rosters but no prediction; MileSplit's team-score is paywalled). A working taste — not a locked card — is the thing a coach screenshots and shares. Reuses existing code, so leverage is enormous.
- **[P0/medium]** Pre-load a demo squad (6–8 anonymous athletes) so the roster magic renders in ~5 seconds with zero typing, plus a 60-second bulk paste (name+time, one per line / spreadsheet paste) for anonymous users to drop their own squad and instantly see equivalent performances, event coverage, 1-5 spread and training-load flags.
  - _Why:_ An unknown brand must DEMONSTRATE the value before a coach types anything; pre-loaded sample + sub-minute import beats every incumbent on time-to-value and closes the trust gap for a no-name tool.
- **[P1/low]** Make the calculator track/XC-native and outcome-framed: keep 800/1600/1mi/3200/5K/10K (already present), lead with an outcome line ('Race-ready paces for every athlete on your roster — in seconds') instead of 'Enter a race time. Get training paces.', and name the methodology (VDOT / Riegel / critical-speed) inline so the math is trusted.
  - _Why:_ Free road predictors botch sub-5K track events; 'speaks track' instantly signals built-for-HS-coaches. Cited science earns trust an unknown brand otherwise lacks.
- **[P1/low]** Keep the PRO wall at the deep stuff only: unlimited roster size, save/sync across devices, squad-wide heat/altitude/XC-course adjustment, PDF team report/lineup card, and the lineup/event-fit optimizer. Free stays genuinely useful and un-nagged.
  - _Why:_ Gives free users more than any incumbent free tier while preserving a clear, honest upgrade reason (own your data + scale + environmental adjustment), not a crippled teaser.
- **[P2/low]** Add an explicit minors'-data privacy line and 'no ads, no selling athlete data' note on the front door.
  - _Why:_ Non-negotiable trust signal for HS programs handling kids' data; cheap to add, closes a hard objection.

## Motion plan
- **[P0]** Free-calculator result + Squad Intelligence KPI/score tiles (renderFreeTierCalculator ~index.html:4256, renderSquadAnalytics tiles ~5527) → GSAP count-up tween on a numeric proxy + onUpdate text write (Whoop/Oura 'premium health app' moment). Tiny code, high payoff, fires once on render/refresh.
- **[P0]** Global, before any motion ships → Single prefers-reduced-motion kill-switch: zero all durations, skip View Transitions, pause the WebGL canvas. Treat reduced-motion as a first-class render path (file currently has only one such block).
- **[P0]** Buttons, nav items, rows, tabs, chips, preset pills → Pure-CSS transform+opacity transitions, 120–250ms, compositor-only. No JS. Keeps expert navigation snappy.
- **[P1]** View/tab swaps inside goTo() (index.html ~2745) → Wrap the screen swap in document.startViewTransition() with a feature-check no-op fallback; scope view-transition-name to stable chrome only, bypass on the big roster/workouts tables. 0KB, biggest polish-per-byte win.
- **[P1]** Signed-out landing hero (new 'home' screen) → GSAP staggered entrance (eyebrow→headline→subhead→CTA ladder→proof line) + one subtle orange accent-glow pulse. The 'first 10 seconds feel alive' moment.
- **[P1]** Cards/sections as they scroll into view (landing + Coach Tools) → CSS scroll-driven animation-timeline: view() reveal — off-main-thread, immune to table-render jank. Pure progressive enhancement: elements visible by default, animation only where supported.
- **[P2]** Roster grid on sort/filter/regroup (renderRoster) → GSAP Flip (lazy-loaded on roster view only) — transforms-only FLIP so reshuffles are trackable, not a jarring repaint.
- **[P2]** Landing/hero header band ONLY — never behind data → One Stripe-style WebGL mesh-gradient canvas (~10KB whatamesh/miniGl), DPR capped ~1.5, paused via IntersectionObserver when offscreen and under reduced-motion. The 'future of coaching' signature shimmer at near-zero CPU.

## Risks / guardrails
- Performance: animate ONLY transform/opacity (plus filter/clip-path for glass+gradient). Never animate width/height/top/left/box-shadow/background-position on data UI — layout-triggering animation is the #1 jank source in a table-heavy SPA.
- View Transitions snapshot the whole changed subtree — scope view-transition-name to stable chrome and EXCLUDE the big roster/workouts/training-log tables; bypass entirely above a row-count threshold.
- Reduced-motion is a first-class path, not an afterthought: one global guard must zero durations, skip startViewTransition, pause the WebGL canvas, and disable count-ups.
- Don't ship two animation engines for the same job — GSAP is already loaded; only add Motion-mini (2.6KB) if you MEASURE GSAP tween stutter under a full roster, and only for the stuttering tween. No React libs (there's no React).
- Wire budget: page is already ~380KB HTML + Supabase + PapaParse + XLSX. Lazy-load Flip/SplitText/WebGL so they don't delay first interaction; keep added animation JS under ~25KB gz; pin CDN deps with SRI.
- Free Squad taste must be capped and abuse-resistant: ≤6 athletes, in-memory only for anonymous users, no write to Supabase without a session — don't let the free path create coach rows or bypass RLS.
- Trial migration safety: use CREATE OR REPLACE in a NEW migration (never edit applied migrations); the backfill +16 days must filter trial_ends_at > now() so it never resurrects already-expired trials. Confirm pg_cron extension is enabled before scheduling expire_trials, and that the function is owned/security-defined appropriately.
- Don't over-rotate into a marketing brochure: the DNA research is explicit that the software sibling should NOT copy anthoneyq's persuasion-arc scroll story — keep 'home' compact and put the live tool above the fold. Demote the retired marketing pink in favor of state colors only.
- Keep durations short/tasteful (120–350ms, ease-out) on app surfaces; reserve magnetic-button / kinetic word-split / 1.7s chart-draw flourishes for the landing only — springy/bouncy motion on an analytics tool reads as toy-like and slows expert users.
- Measure on a mid-tier laptop with the largest realistic roster loaded; target sustained 60fps and <16ms frames during a roster sort. If any animation can't hold 60fps with real data, cut it.
