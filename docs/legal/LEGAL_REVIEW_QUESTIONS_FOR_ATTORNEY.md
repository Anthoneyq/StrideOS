# STRIDE OS Legal Review Questions

**Purpose:** Focus an attorney review on the issues that matter before broader paid school/team sales.
**Last updated:** July 2, 2026

---

- `/terms.html`
- `/privacy.html`
- `docs/legal/STUDENT_DATA_PRIVACY_SUMMARY.md`

- Stripe checkout/subscription flow and cancellation language
- Supabase data model for coach profiles, athletes, billing, export, deletion, and under-13 handling

## Highest-priority questions

1. What legal entity should appear in the Terms, Privacy Policy, quotes, invoices, and W-9?
2. What state law, venue, arbitration, limitation-of-liability, and indemnity language should be used?
3. Is the current coach-signup consent language enough for a beta paid launch?
4. What COPPA notice/consent workflow is required if coaches enter data for athletes under 13?
5. For school customers, should STRIDE OS position itself as a "school official" / service provider under FERPA, and what written controls are required?
6. What state student privacy laws apply to the likely launch states?
7. What data processing addendum or student data privacy agreement template should be offered to schools/districts?
8. Are the product claims about forecasts, paces, lineup optimization, and training insights adequately substantiated and disclaimed?
9. Do any health, injury, wellness, recovery, HRV, or readiness features change the legal posture?
10. What insurance, incident-response notice, and breach-notification commitments should be made before district-level sales?

## Product decisions that need legal input

- Whether under-13 athletes are allowed at launch, and if so under what authorization path.
- Whether student/athlete login is allowed or delayed.
- Whether de-identified/aggregate research use should be opt-in, opt-out, or unavailable until a formal research agreement exists.
- Whether parent requests should be routed through the school/team or handled directly by STRIDE OS.
- Whether coach-entered initials/IDs should be recommended by default for cautious schools.
- Whether any "medical", "health", "recovery", "injury risk", or "readiness" language must be removed or narrowed.
