## What STRIDE OS is

STRIDE OS is roster intelligence software for track and cross country coaches. Coaches enter or import race marks and roster data, then STRIDE OS helps turn that information into training paces, event-fit decisions, race forecasts, printable team sheets, and meet-lineup guidance.

STRIDE OS is not a public results site, social media platform, medical tool, or meet-timing system.

## Why a school or team would use it

Most track and cross country programs already have race results scattered across spreadsheets, result sites, notebooks, and coach memory. The hard part is not storing another result. The hard part is answering coach decisions quickly:

- Which athletes should train together this week?
- Which athlete is better suited for the 800m, 1600m, 3200m, or 5K?
- Which paces are appropriate for the whole roster, not just one athlete?
- Which meet lineup gives the team the strongest event coverage?
- How can coaches explain decisions to athletes, staff, and parents without rebuilding the same spreadsheet every week?

STRIDE OS is built around those decisions.

## How it differs from common alternatives

| Alternative | Useful for | Limitation STRIDE OS addresses |
|---|---|---|
| Spreadsheet | Flexible manual tracking | Requires the coach to build formulas, copy data, and maintain reports by hand. |
| VDOT/McMillan-style calculator | Individual paces and race equivalents | Usually one athlete and one race input at a time; not built around a full roster. |
| Athletic.net/MileSplit/TFRRS | Results, rankings, rosters, meet history | Primarily data/result systems, not private coach-decision tools. |
| Training log platforms | Plans, calendars, device sync | Often focused on daily training delivery rather than track/XC event-fit and roster decisions. |
| Meet management/timing software | Entries, timing, results publication | Designed for running the meet, not week-to-week coaching decisions. |

## Current product strengths

- Coach-managed roster workflow.
- CSV/XLSX/paste import support for roster and meet-result workflows.
- Multi-event athlete profiles where more than one race mark exists.
- Training paces across coach-readable intensity zones.
- Race forecasts with planning ranges and confidence language.
- Event-fit analysis for track/XC events.
- Printable pace cards, group sheets, and lineup sheets.
- Data export and account-deletion workflows for signed-in coaches.
- No ads and no sale of athlete data.
- Student data can be managed by the coach without requiring student accounts.

## Honest limits

STRIDE OS is decision-support software. It does not replace coach judgment and does not guarantee outcomes. Forecasts and lineup guidance depend on the quality, recency, and completeness of the data entered. Current confidence/range language is for planning and trust-building; it should not be represented as a fully calibrated statistical guarantee.

## School-friendly posture

STRIDE OS is designed to support a school or district review process:

- Athlete data is used to provide the service.
- Athlete data is not sold.
- The product does not run ads.
- Coach-managed roster use does not require student accounts.
- Export and deletion workflows are available.
- Subprocessors are documented in `SUBPROCESSORS.md`.
